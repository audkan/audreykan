TO DO

Golden Gate Park

Rent tandem bikes or a 3-6 person surrey and ride around the park. Rent a row or pedal boat at Stow Lake. Check out all the museums in the park too!

The San Francisco Piers

Walk from the Bay Bridge to Fisherman's Wharf. Make sure to stop at Rincon Park, the Ferry Building, and Pier 7 on the way!

Dolores Park

Best place to have a picnic. Grab ice cream at Bi-Rite, drinks at a corner store, and a burrito nearby in the Mission.

Crissy Fields

Grab an electric bike or scooter off the street to explore the Marina, Fort Mason, Ghirardelli Square, and Maritime Park.

Ride the Cable Cars

Power/Hyde is the best route of the three (Powell/Hyde, Powell/Mason, and California). Buy an All-Day Passport for $14 and enjoy the ride!

Pearl Spa and Sauna

Spend a morning or afternoon relaxing at a Korean-style ladies-only health spa

TO DO

Pike Place Market

Spend the day grabbing small bites around the market and head to the waterfront afterwards! Personal favorites: Truffle Queen to sample truffle oil and spreads, Jack's Fish Spot for raw oysters, and City Fish Co. for $5 scallop sashimi

Fremont Sunday Market

This weekly market has a bunch of things to see and eat: there are antiques, art, clothes, tools & junk as well as food trucks selling a variety of street food. Fremont is also home to a lot of quirky boutiques and stores!

TO DO

Bike the Bays

Rent a bike in Auckland CBD and head to Mission Bay (St. Heliers). Stop for a quick swim or picnic at the beach. Try some hot chips (aka fries) and Hokey Pokey flavored ice cream at the convenience store!

Silo Park

Head to this inner city oasis by the waterfront. Check out the Silo Bar, food trucks, local DJs, outdoor cinema, and outdoor sculptures.

Gibbs Farm (Sculpture Walk)

Don't miss this larger-than-life, open-air collection of commissioned art. (Tickets usually sell out, so book ahead.) The surreal artworks are held on 990-acres of land and are large enough to be seen from satellite imagery!

TO DO

Museumplein

Check out all the museums in Museum Quarter: Rijksmuseum, Van Gogh Museum, MOCO Museum, and Stedelijk Museum. This is also where the famous I AMSTERADAM sculpture is held!

Tram Day Pass

Purchase a day pass and sight-see from the window-seat! Allocate a day to ride each tram from end to end. Trust me, it's the best way to see Amsterdam and all it's neighborhoods!

Zaandijk Zaanse Schans (and Zaandam)

Spend a day in this neighborhood known for its preservation of Dutch cultural heritage. On the way back to Amsterdam, stop by Zaandam to check out the crazy architecture of Inntel Hotels.
